#01 – Self Portrait. First serious interaction with coloured pencil & finished piece in that medium. 5.8 x 8.3in.


#02 – Egg Priest. Digital Colour. Fearing what was overhead – without knowing the risks, the egg priest took the last of the pupils and headed down the steps towards the village.


#03 – Queen/Chief. Digital Colour. Queen of the snake clan. Friend of the lotus divers & bead merchants.


#04 – Jelly Hand. Unease. Sense of change. Grasp on the situation weakening. Pressure rising, feeling heavy. Everything becoming lucid.


#05 – Life of a Boar. First proper learning experience at using proper tools in relief print making. Woodcut Relief Print – Oil-Based Black.


#06 – Death of a Boar. Small imagery created to accompany Life of a Boar. Woodcut Relief Print – Black.


#07 – Ceremony March. Natural born image from the storytelling that occured between Life and Death of a Boar, with additional research into 16th century relief printing. Woodcut Relief Print – Black.


#08 – Man with Pipe. Cut with the intention of adding texture and shading. Linocut Relief Print – Black.


#09 – Box of Matches. Based from various loose pencil studies of a matchbox. Linocut Relief Print – Crimson.


#10 – Widow and Orphan. Exercise in visual storytelling to get out of an artistic rut. Woodcut Relief Print – Water-Based Black, Water-Based Crimson.
